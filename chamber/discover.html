About Addis Ababa

In 2024, the population of Addis Ababa is projected to be approximately 4.8 million people in the urban area and 2.7 million in the core city area. This marks a significant growth from previous decades, reflecting its status as Ethiopia's capital and largest city. Gender distribution in 2022 was estimated at about 47% men and 53% women, totaling roughly 1.82 million males and 2.04 million females. Over the years, Addis Ababa has experienced rapid urbanization, and its diverse population includes a mix of Ethiopia's major ethnic groups. The city remains central to Ethiopia's economy and governance and plays a key role in African politics as the headquarters of the African Union.

International Sales in 2024

In 2024, the Addis Chamber of Commerce hosted the 26th edition of the Addis-Chamber International Trade Fair (ACITF), a key event aimed at modernizing Ethiopia's trade sector. The fair, held at the Addis Ababa Exhibition Center, featured a mix of local and international participants, showcasing products and services from 60 Ethiopian trade associations and six international companies. This year's fair aimed to boost market linkages for Ethiopian manufacturers and foster collaboration between local and foreign industries. Although participation faced challenges due to limited exhibition space, the Addis Chamber emphasized its commitment to addressing these issues and enhancing the event's vibrancy in future editions

Ethiopia Land of Origin

Ethiopia is a country like no other, offering a rich tapestry of history, culture, and natural wonders. From ancient civilizations to its distinct traditions, Ethiopia's uniqueness resonates in every aspect of its identity.

What Makes Ethiopia Stand Out:

  • Cradle of Humanity: Fossils like Lucy (*Australopithecus afarensis*) and Ardi (*Ardipithecus ramidus*) were discovered here, showcasing Ethiopia's role in human evolution.
  • Ancient Civilizations: The Aksumite Empire, an influential trading hub, and early adopter of Christianity, highlights Ethiopia's historical significance.
  • Unique Script and Language: Ethiopia uses the Ge'ez alphabet, making Amharic one of the few African languages with its own writing system.
  • Uncolonized Legacy: Ethiopia's victory over Italy at the Battle of Adwa symbolizes resistance against colonization, making it the only African country never colonized.
  • Religious Diversity: Home to the Ethiopian Orthodox Church and significant Muslim communities, the country thrives on religious harmony.
  • Coffee Origin: Ethiopia is the birthplace of coffee, with its cultural ceremonies celebrating this global favorite.
  • Stunning Geography: From the highlands to the Danakil Depression, Ethiopia&aposs diverse landscape includes some of the hottest and lowest places on Earth.
  • UNESCO Sites: Lalibela&aposs rock-hewn churches and the Simien Mountains are just a few of Ethiopia&aposs World Heritage Sites.
